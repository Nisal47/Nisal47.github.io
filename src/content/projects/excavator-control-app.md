## Overview

I used Figma Make to generate and shape a four-screen tablet interface for an excavator operator: Control, Camera, Diagnostics, and Settings. It has a consistent dark UI, live machine readouts, camera views with a depth overlay, a tiered alert log, and adaptive operator profiles. From idea to a clickable, coherent layout in one short session, this is a concept we could take into co-design sessions with operators to shape an in-cab interface that's genuinely usable and fit for purpose.

## Where It Stands

The surface is polished, but this is an early, unvalidated draft, and that shows in a few places. The Control screen drives boom, stick, swing, and bucket from touchscreen joysticks, and puts the E-STOP on the screen too. Real machines use physical joysticks for feel and fine control, and a real emergency stop is hardwired; neither belongs on glass. Smaller issues: status relies on colour alone, which could be hard to read for colour-blind operators or in bright sun, and Diagnostics reports "nominal" while a critical alert is active. It's a clean example of AI getting you to something plausible fast, ready to be sharpened with user-centred design.

## How I'd Improve It

Bring in user-centred design. Watch real operators on site, talk to them, show them the concept early and get their first impressions. Map their actual tasks, then test the design with gloves on, in sunlight, with the machine running, measuring workload, usability, experience, and effectiveness.

## Designing for a Construction Cab

The constraints that should drive every decision: the operator's eyes belong on the work, not the screen; gloves and vibration demand large, forgiving targets; the display must stay readable in direct sun and through dust; alerts can't rely on sound or colour alone; and safety-critical warnings need a clear, honest hierarchy.

## Takeaway

AI got me to a coherent design fast, and that speed is the real win: instead of spending early sessions explaining a concept from scratch, operators can react to something concrete and help refine it from day one. Used this way, AI is not a shortcut around good design, it's a faster way into it.
