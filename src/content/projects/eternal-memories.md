## Overview

In New Zealand, palliative care is a core part of end-of-life support, aiming to protect a patient's quality of life through physical, emotional, and spiritual care for both patients and their families. As illness progresses, though, patients often lose the ability to be in the places that mattered most to them, and family members, especially those living overseas or unable to visit often, can feel shut out of the small, everyday moments of connection.

Eternal Memories, built by our team XR Minds at XR Workshop 2023 (hosted by HIT Lab NZ at the University of Canterbury, in collaboration with the Empathic Computing Lab at the University of Auckland and the HCI Group at Victoria University of Wellington), set out to close that gap. The idea was simple but personal: rebuild a patient's favourite home space in VR, populate it with the photos, music, videos, and objects that mattered to them, and let family step into that space, whether they were in the room or on the other side of the world, to add to it and leave messages behind.

## Process

Our prototype was built within 15 hours during XR Workshop 2023. Given that timeframe, we prioritised a small set of features that would make the emotional core of the idea tangible rather than trying to build everything at once.

The result combined VR and AR into one connected system. In VR, patients and family could share the same virtual space simultaneously, customise it in real time, and browse a photo memory wall built from the patient's own images. In AR, family members could leave messages that persisted in physical space for the patient to find later, effectively letting a patient "post" time-capsule messages for family to open after they'd gone. We added hand-tracked interactive pets in AR for a lighter, more comforting touch, an AR spectator app so people without a headset could still watch and follow along with what was happening in VR, and voice chat linking AR and VR users so conversations could flow naturally across both.

Working across VR and AR simultaneously, under hackathon time pressure, meant constant trade-offs, so we leaned on rapid prototyping and cut scope aggressively to keep the experience emotionally coherent rather than feature-complete.

## Outcome / Impact

Eternal Memories placed 3rd overall at XR Workshop 2023, judged against every team at the event. More than the placing, it was a proof of concept that a shared, persistent XR memory space is achievable even under extreme time constraints, and that the emotional impact of the idea (letting a patient give their favourite place to their family, rather than the other way around) resonated with both judges and other participants.

The project reinforced for me how quickly meaningful HCI/XR concepts can be validated with the right scoping, and it's part of what continues to shape my interest in applying immersive technology to real, human-centred problems rather than purely technical ones.
